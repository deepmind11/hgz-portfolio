# Experience: AI Biology Trainer — Outlier (Scale AI)

From January 2024 to September 2024, concurrent with his Columbia research role, Harshit worked part-time and remote as an AI Biology Trainer for Outlier, the Scale AI platform for domain-expert training data. Scale AI provides training data infrastructure for frontier AI labs including OpenAI, Google DeepMind, and Meta.

**Prompt Engineering.** Designed and wrote biology-domain prompts and questions across molecular biology, genomics, biochemistry, computational biology, and bioinformatics to test and elicit LLM capabilities. This work contributed to the input side of RLHF training pipelines at Scale AI.

**Model Evaluation.** Rated LLM-generated responses and assessed adherence to system instructions. He evaluated scientific accuracy, response completeness, reasoning quality, and prompt-following behavior to provide structured human feedback for foundation model alignment.

**Hallucination Detection.** Identified and flagged scientific hallucinations, factual errors, logical reasoning failures, and citation fabrications across hundreds of biology-domain interactions, contributing to training data quality for large-scale AI systems.

This role gave Harshit direct hands-on experience with how foundation models are evaluated and aligned, which informs his current work building agentic AI systems for genomics and clinical operations.

Keywords: RLHF, large language models, LLMs, foundation models, AI alignment, prompt engineering, prompt evaluation, human feedback, model evaluation, hallucination detection, instruction following, AI training data.
