# Publication

Harshit is a co-author on the paper "Record High Magnetic Anisotropy in Three-Coordinate Mn(III) and Cr(II) Complexes: A Theoretical Perspective," published in Inorganic Chemistry by the American Chemical Society. DOI: 10.1021/acs.inorgchem.1c00978.

The work originated in his summer 2017 research internship at the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Bombay during his undergraduate studies. The project performed ab initio quantum chemistry and Density Functional Theory (DFT) calculations on a high-spin d4 tri-coordinate Mn(III) complex, identifying magneto-structural correlations that explain the origin of record-high magnetic anisotropy in single-molecule magnets.

This is his only first-authorship-track publication; his current work in bioinformatics and AI engineering is focused on production systems and open-source projects rather than academic publications.
