# Work authorization

Harshit is currently on an H-1B visa, which is transferable to a new employer. H-1B transfers are routine for any company that has filed an H-1B petition before — most established US tech and biotech companies have an immigration attorney on retainer and handle transfers in days to weeks via premium processing. Under H-1B portability rules, an employee can begin working for a new employer once the transfer petition is filed (receipt notice), even before final USCIS approval.

For employers, the transfer process is straightforward: the company files Form I-129 with USCIS, pays the standard filing fees, files a Labor Condition Application with the Department of Labor, and the employee can start as soon as the receipt notice is issued. Premium processing brings approval to 15 business days. Most employers with H-1B experience opt for premium processing for transfers.

Harshit can only accept full-time, W-2 employment. He cannot do 1099 contract work, freelance, part-time, or contract-to-hire roles. He is authorized to work for any US employer that is willing to file an H-1B transfer petition, which is the standard arrangement.

He is also potentially eligible for green card sponsorship in EB-2 or EB-1B categories, depending on the employer.
